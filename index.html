Executive Summary

The decision-maker view

Business question

Why do industrial jobs remain hard to fill in AURA even as unemployment falls — and how can AI improve matching without simply accelerating flawed recruitment filters?

Main conclusion

The central problem is not a lack of available workers. It is a structural mismatch between job requirements, candidate profiles, and the way traditional ATS systems screen applications.

Strategic takeaway

Recruitment systems should move from binary keyword filtering to graded, explainable talent evaluation combined with proactive rediscovery of near-match candidates.

Hackathon Context

What the challenge asked us to solve

Challenge brief

The 2026 ESSCA Data Hackathon focused on the Auvergne-Rhône-Alpes region and asked participants to explore how AI could help address persistent job market mismatches, not just through technical automation, but by incorporating the broader social, training, cultural, and geographic reasons why needs remain unmet.

Evaluation criteria

Teams were evaluated on originality, feasibility, data robustness, and overall relevance to the real labour market problem — which made solution framing and practical fit just as important as analytical insight.

Research & Market Findings

What the AURA analysis revealed

Unemployment is falling

AURA unemployment dropped from roughly 327K to 293K in about 18 months, yet industrial roles remain persistently difficult to fill.

Industrial hiring is still broken

The region still faces 8,000+ industrial jobs left unfilled each year and an average industrial recruitment difficulty rate of 61.5%.

Difficulty is structural, not temporary

Clustering of jobs by demand and recruitment difficulty identified roughly 60 structurally hard-to-fill jobs, showing that the mismatch is systemic rather than random.

Core paradox: unemployment is falling, yet industrial vacancies remain hard to fill. This confirms that the issue is not simple labour scarcity — it is the failure of current matching systems to connect available workers with viable roles.

Why Traditional ATS Systems Fail

The recruitment bottleneck behind the mismatch

Binary filtering logic

Traditional ATS tools still rely heavily on Boolean keyword matching and rigid eligibility rules. This produces binary outcomes: either a candidate passes, or is discarded.

  • Resumes can be rejected before human review.
  • Formatting and keyword absence can trigger exclusion.
  • Near-match candidates are often eliminated instead of evaluated.

Double penalty for industrial workers

Industrial candidates are especially vulnerable because their CVs may be “keyword-poor” by nature and certifications may be expired even when they are quickly renewable. This means viable profiles can be screened out for reasons that do not reflect actual trainability or job potential.

Observed issue What it means in practice Impact on hiring
Boolean keyword matching ATS checks exact terms and predefined requirements instead of broader relevance. Viable candidates are excluded too early.
Binary pass/fail logic Systems cannot express “almost fit” or “trainable with minor gaps”. Recruiters lose access to near-match talent pools.
No explainability Candidates and recruiters do not know what is missing or how close the profile is. No learning loop, no retraining path, no recovery mechanism.

Proposed Solution

Two functionalities, one closed-loop system

01 — ATS FairMatch Layer

A plug-in layer sitting between Applications → ATS → Recruiter, designed to replace binary filtering with a graded, explainable evaluation model.

  • Fit score (0–100)
  • Explainability: why the score is what it is
  • Gap plan: what is missing and how long it may take to close

02 — Talent Re-Discovery Engine

A proactive outreach layer that scans the regional unemployed talent pool, identifies viable near-match profiles, and invites them to apply when a company receives too few relevant CVs for a critical role.

  • Searches unemployed talent pools in AURA
  • Finds potential profiles even if not “perfect”
  • Sends opportunity alerts and application invitations
  • Loops candidates back into the FairMatch layer
Integrated logic: the Talent Re-Discovery Engine finds who to look at, while the ATS FairMatch Layer decides how to evaluate them. Together, they transform recruitment failure into structured decision-making.

How the System Evaluates Candidates

From rigid rejection to structured decision support

Inputs

  • CV (structured or free text)
  • Job description (structured or free text)
  • Policy rules distinguishing mandatory vs preferred requirements

Outputs

  • Fit score from 0 to 100
  • Explainability: top drivers and blockers
  • Gap plan: what is missing and expected time to close

Business Impact

Why this matters beyond the hackathon

Reduce talent loss

By capturing near-match candidates instead of discarding them, employers regain access to profiles that would otherwise never reach human review.

Improve recruiter decision quality

Fit scoring, explainability, and gap plans create a more nuanced decision framework than binary ATS outputs.

Support reskilling logic

The system does not only say “no”. It identifies what is missing and frames trainability as part of the hiring decision.

Why this is relevant to AURA

The solution is directly aligned with the region’s paradox: workers exist, jobs exist, but connection mechanisms are failing. By focusing on structurally hard-to-fill jobs, the model targets the part of the market where the impact is most urgent.

Why this is strategically strong

This is not just an HR automation idea. It combines labour market analytics, system redesign, explainable AI, and fairness-aware decision support — making it relevant for HR tech, workforce strategy, and public-private employment innovation.

Ethical Value

Why AI should not make unfair screening faster

Fairer candidate visibility

The solution reduces exclusion caused by brittle rules and limited keyword logic, making recruitment more transparent and less dependent on formatting or exact lexical matches.

Explainability over opacity

Rather than hiding rejection behind black-box filters, the model gives recruiters and candidates a clearer understanding of blockers, gaps, and possible paths forward.
